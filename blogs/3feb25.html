The Importance of Menstrual Hygiene Management in India
Written by: Muskan Choraria

Menstrual Hygiene Management (MHM) plays a crucial role in the health, well-being, and dignity of women and girls, especially in India. Unfortunately, a lack of awareness about menstrual health is still prevalent in rural areas, affecting a large number of women and young girls. It goes beyond using products; it involves creating awareness, eliminating taboos, and providing access to necessary services.

Menstruation remains a highly secluded topic, with women in many regions lacking adequate information on hygiene. This ignorance leads to health risks, including infections and other medical complications. Menstrual hygiene management should be prioritized in rural areas where infrastructure to support proper practices is inadequate. Many girls do not have access to clean, private spaces during menstruation, which only adds to their struggle.

Several menstrual health awareness campaigns are being conducted across the country to educate women and girls about safe menstrual products, their disposal, and the effects of poor hygiene. These initiatives aim to make menstruation a topic of open discussion, encouraging safe practices. Global organizations and the government are also actively involved in spreading this message.

Proper menstrual management helps prevent infections such as urinary tract infections (UTIs) and other reproductive health issues. Inadequate hygiene practices may lead to serious health problems like cervical cancer and infertility. Additionally, improper disposal of menstrual products causes environmental pollution, which needs to be addressed.

Awareness campaigns emphasize the importance of knowing how to use sanitary products correctly and disposing of them safely. They seek to educate women, particularly in rural areas, on the importance of menstrual hygiene and its long-term effects on their health and the environment.

The ongoing ignorance surrounding menstrual hygiene has adversely impacted the welfare of women. However, awareness campaigns focusing on education, accessibility to safe menstrual products, and breaking taboos are making a positive difference.
